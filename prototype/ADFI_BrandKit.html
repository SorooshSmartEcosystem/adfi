03 · TYPOGRAPHY

two faces. two weights. always lowercase.

SF Pro for prose. SF Mono for numbers and timestamps. nothing else. always sentence case — the brand sounds like a friend, not a billboard.

weight rule: only 400 (regular) and 500 (medium). never bold (700) — too aggressive for the brand voice. never light (300) — looks fragile.
04 · USAGE

how to use the orb.

the orb has personality — keep it intact. these rules protect it.

✓ DO
give the orb space. minimum padding equal to half its diameter on all sides. the orb is the focal point — let it breathe.
adfi
✗ DON'T
don't crowd the orb against the wordmark. always at least 14px of breathing room between orb and text in lockups.
✓ DO
keep the orb glossy and round. the highlight in the upper-left and the soft shadow underneath are part of the identity.
✗ DON'T
don't flatten the orb to a black circle. without the gloss it loses its character — feels lifeless. only flatten below 16px display size.
✓ DO
on dark backgrounds, add a hairline outer ring (0.5px white at 6% opacity) to keep the orb's edge defined.
✗ DON'T
don't recolor the orb. ever. not for holidays, not for partner co-branding, not for product variants. the orb is always black.
animation: when on screen, the orb should breathe — a slow 3-second scale loop from 1.0 to 1.04. this is part of the identity, not a decoration. static orbs are fine in print.
05 · VOICE

adfi sounds like a colleague.

not a tool, not a service, not "an ai-powered platform." a colleague. always first-person, always lowercase, always honest about what was done.

✗ NOT THIS
"3 posts published successfully."
✓ ADFI VOICE
"i caught a missed call. booked her for thursday."
✗ NOT THIS
"call handled. appointment scheduled."
✓ ADFI VOICE
"give me 30 seconds. i'll see what i can find."
✗ NOT THIS
"loading... please wait while we process your request."
✓ ADFI VOICE
"one thing needs you."
✗ NOT THIS
"1 action item requires your attention."
✓ ADFI VOICE
"hire me."
✗ NOT THIS
"start your free trial today!"
internal terms stay internal. users never see "agent", "model", "llm", "endpoint", "vector", "queue", "context window." they hired adfi — one colleague — they don't see the team behind the curtain.
